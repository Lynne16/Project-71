school achievements

Inner School Debate on Merits and Demerits of Democracy
Won first place on the inner school debate on Merits and Demerits of Democracy

Debates were held in class 9 on the topic ‘Merits and Demerits of Democracy’ and on ‘Was the British Rule helpful to Indians?’ in class 10. The students were divided into two teams of 6 students each and the rest of the class acted as the audience. Both teams presented their views and points of order. After that the arguments in favour and against were put forth and argued. There were very interesting opinions and viewpoints. In class 9 the students learnt about our democratic system and in class 10 about British History. It showed us the importance of the Colonial Rule. The audience questioned and voted in favour of the best team. Students of VVA do a lot of reseach on the topic and present very heated debates.

Inter Class Football Tournament
Won first place on the inner school debate on Merits and Demerits of Democracy

The inter-class football tournament of class 8A v/s 8B was conducted on 07th July 2012 on the VVA School Ground. It was a seven aside match. Despite heavy rain the students participated in good spirit. In the first half, 8B boys scored 01 goal while 8B was nil. In the second half 8B scored yet another goal and won the match. Both goals were scored by Ashier Fernandes of 8B.

  • ACADEMIC - LACK OF PROBITY

    Any time that the pupil misrepresents academic performance amounts to being dishonest: 1. Copying from another pupil during a test or an examination. 2. Allowing other pupils to copy from one's own work, be it an assignment, project, test or examination. 3. Arranging with other pupils to give or receive answers through signals, during a test or examination. 4. Using or possessing any written material, books or information that could be used during the test or examination. 5. Failing to comply with the rules for the procedures to be followed during a test / examination such as not talking to others, being in one's place, observing silence in the test / examination room, keeping all bags outside the exam room / hall. 6. Attempting to change scores or grades or altering marks or any answers on graded sheets returned to the pupils. 7. Use of plagiarism: Representing work done by others as one's own work / citing a fictitious source in a submitted work / failing to cite the sources in a submitted assignment / project.
